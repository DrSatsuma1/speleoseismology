**Their finding**: No effect on cave atmosphere or drip rates from M7.4 earthquake.

**Why your hypothesis still holds** (must articulate in any outreach):

| Ridley 2015 | Your Hypothesis |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Short-term drip rates (hours-days) | Long-term isotope anomalies (decades) |
| Distant subduction quake (offshore Guatemala) | Nearby strike-slip rupture (Motagua, ~100 km) |
| Immediate hydrology response | Chiodini CO2 flux model (years-decades) |
| Cave atmosphere monitoring | Aquifer disruption / fracture network changes |

**Key argument**: Ridley studied IMMEDIATE effects on HYDROLOGY. You're proposing LONG-TERM effects on GEOCHEMISTRY via different mechanism (geogenic CO2 flux through newly opened fractures).
