### Bunker Cave 826 CE - **REMAINS DARK EARTHQUAKE CANDIDATE** (Archaeological correlation rejected)

**⚠️ STATUS UPDATE (2026-01-02): Archaeological correlation WITHDRAWN**

The Cologne Praetorium damage originally attributed to an 800-840 AD earthquake (Hinzen & Schütte, 2003) was **re-evaluated** by Hinzen et al. (2013, J Seismol 17:399-424). Their comprehensive analysis concluded:

> "A seismogenic cause of all the damages is **less probable** than previously hypothesized... we favor the interpretation of **seepage effects and subsurface erosion**"

**Plausibility Matrix (Hinzen et al. 2013)**:
| Damage Cause | Plausibility Index |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **Subsurface erosion** | **0.82** (favored) |
| Seismogenic | 0.59 |
| Slow static settlement | 0.36 |

**Radiocarbon dates from Cologne** (809-980 calCE, 855-975 calCE) overlap with Bunker Cave anomaly timing, but damage attributed to:
1. Failure of Roman drainage system after 4th century CE
2. Repeated heavy rainstorms causing subsurface suffusion
3. WWII bombing (sewer roof crack)

**Bunker Cave 826 CE remains a dark earthquake candidate** based on:
- Z-score: +1.48σ
- Recovery: 52 years (above climatic threshold)
- Tectonic setting: Lower Rhine Graben is seismically active

**Classification**: **SEISMIC (MODERATE)** - no independent validation

**Lesson learned**: Archaeological damage correlations require careful evaluation - multiple causes can produce similar damage patterns.

## Validation Attempts (2026-01-02)

### Te Reinga Cave (New Zealand) - 989 CE: SUGGESTIVE but NOT VALIDATED

**Paleoseismic search results** ([Hikurangi margin studies](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0277379124004438)):
- Hikurangi margin event at ~870-815 BP (1080-1135 CE) from integrated onshore-offshore records
- Opoho Valley tsunami deposit at ~900 BP (~1050 CE)
- **Temporal offset**: 60-90 years from Te Reinga 989 CE anomaly

**Chiodini model validation**:
| Parameter | Predicted (M8 @ 113 km) | Observed |
|-----------|-------------------------|----------|
| Recovery time | 63 years | 47 years |
| CO2 flux perturbation | 260% | — |
| Detectable? | Yes | Yes (z=-1.83σ) |

**Conclusion**: The 60-90 year offset exceeds typical radiocarbon uncertainties (~30-50 years). While the recovery time match is reasonable and Te Reinga is within detection range of Hikurangi megathrusts, the temporal mismatch prevents confident validation. Classification remains **MODERATE**.

### Uluu-Too Cave (Kyrgyzstan) - 510 CE: DISTANCE TOO FAR

**Paleoseismic search results** ([Kultor Fault trenches](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1134/S0001433817070064)):
- Kultor adyr fault (Issyk-Kul region): earthquakes at 60-170, **530-620**, 1160-1250, 1410-1460 CE
- **530-620 CE window overlaps 510 CE** within dating uncertainties

**Problem**: Distance from Uluu-Too Cave (40.4°N, 72.35°E) to Kultor/Issyk-Kul faults is **452 km** - far exceeding static strain detection threshold (~50 km for M6-7 events).

**Talas-Fergana Fault** (closer to cave): Events at ~300, 2400, 5000 years BP - no match for 510 CE.

**Conclusion**: While the 530-620 CE paleoseismic window overlaps beautifully with the 510 CE anomaly, the 452 km distance precludes correlation. Would require M>8 event for static strain detection at this range. Classification remains **MODERATE**.
