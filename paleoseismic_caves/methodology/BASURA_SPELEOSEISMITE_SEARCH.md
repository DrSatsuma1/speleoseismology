**Strategy**: "Virtual Trenching" - using cave damage instead of surface fault trenches to date paleoearthquakes via U-Th dating of breakage horizons

## Background: The Virtual Trenching Strategy

**Proven methodology in Italy**:
- **Cola Cave (Central Apennines)**: Galli et al. 2021 dated the 508 AD earthquake using broken stalagmites with U-Th precision
- **Calabria caves**: Pisano et al. 2017 dated 3 paleoearthquakes using tilted/broken formations

**Key principle**: If B√†sura has broken formations at the 1285/1394 event horizons (32.5mm and 27.67mm depths in BA18-4), they can be U-Th dated to confirm seismic origin.

**Why this matters**:
- **If found**: Upgrades 1285/1394 from "Dark Earthquakes" (geochemical only) to "Confirmed Earthquakes" (physical damage)
- **If not found**: Doesn't rule out earthquakes (formations may have survived due to geometry, thickness, or cave location)

### What Speleoseismites Would Add (IF Found)

**Scenario A: Breakage found at 32.5mm and 27.67mm depths**

**Impact**:
1. **Independent physical validation** - confirms seismic origin independent of geochemistry
2. **U-Th dating confirmation** - can re-date breakage horizons to verify 1285/1394 ages
3. **Magnitude constraints** - breakage patterns could constrain earthquake size
4. **Upgrades publication case** - physical + geochemical evidence stronger for Nature/Science
5. **Methodology validation** - demonstrates speleothem paleoseismology across multiple proxy types

**Scenario B: No breakage documented or found**

**Does NOT invalidate 1285/1394 detection** because:
- Geochemical evidence is already Tier 1 (multi-proxy)
- Many factors affect breakage: formation geometry, thickness, cave location, attachment type
- Absence of breakage ‚â† absence of earthquake
- Other caves may have breakage even if B√†sura doesn't

**Scenario C: 1887 breakage found but not 1285/1394**

**Would suggest**:
- 1887 was stronger or closer than 1285/1394
- Formation vulnerability changed over time
- Different rupture characteristics (directivity, frequency content)
- Still valuable for calibrating cave seismic response
