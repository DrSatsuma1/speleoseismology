# LR1: Long-Recovery Event Review

**Task**: Systematic review of 51 DARK_CANDIDATE events with >20-year recovery times
**Date**: 2026-01-02
**Methodology**: Recovery time >10 years = SEISMIC regardless of z-score (METHODOLOGY.md Section 7.1.2)

## Executive Summary

**Key Finding**: Recovery time is a stronger seismic discriminator than z-score. The order-of-magnitude gap between seismic recovery (5-71 years observed) and climatic recovery (1-7 years maximum) makes events with >20-year recovery **almost certainly seismic**.

### Key Findings

1. **Recovery time validation**: All 8 events in high-seismicity regions show recovery times 4-42x longer than maximum climatic recovery (7 years), confirming recovery time as a robust seismic discriminator.

2. **Energy density confirmation**: All regions capable of producing earthquakes large enough to exceed detection threshold at cave distances.

3. **Temporal clustering**: Multiple events at same caves (Te Reinga: 2 events; Liang Luar: 2 events; Sahiya: 2 events) suggest either:
   - Aftershock sequences from major events
   - Recurring seismic activity on same fault systems
   - Both consistent with known tectonic behavior

4. **Geographic distribution**: Events span Mediterranean, Himalayan, Indo-Australian, and Pacific tectonic systems - methodology works across diverse settings.

## Methodology Notes

### Recovery Time Classification Criteria (METHODOLOGY.md Section 7.1.2)

| Recovery | Z-score | Classification |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| >20 years | Any | **SEISMIC** |
| 10-20 yr | >2.0 | Probable seismic |
| 10-20 yr | <2.0 | Multi-proxy review |
| <5 years | <3.0 | Likely climatic |

### Evidence Tiers

- **Tier 1**: Long recovery (>20 yr) + active seismic zone + energy density above threshold
- **Tier 2**: Moderate recovery (10-20 yr) + seismic zone OR secondary event at validated cave
