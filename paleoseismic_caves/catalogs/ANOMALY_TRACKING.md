# PART VII: CARBON ISOTOPE DIAGNOSTIC FRAMEWORK

For future validation, the δ13C proxy can distinguish seismic vs climatic origins:

| Measurement | Seismic Scenario | Climatic Scenario |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **δ13C Shift** | Strong negative spike (soil flush/deep gas) | Minimal shift or slow trend |
| **δ18O Shift** | Rapid change (mixing of deep/new water) | Gradual shift (seasonal/temp) |
| **Trace Elements** | High Sr/Ca or Mg/Ca (rock-water interaction) | Relatively stable ratios |
| **Hydrological Status** | Turbidity pulse in chronicles | Increased flow without sediment |

### Deep CO₂ Component

In Italy's extensional faults (Apennines), earthquakes often release deep-seated CO₂:
- Mantle/Deep CO₂: δ13C = -5‰ to -3‰
- If baseline is ~0‰, shift toward these values = fault has "uncorked" deep reservoir

**CRITICAL CAVEAT**: BA18-4 lacks δ13C data. This framework cannot be applied without new sample analysis.
