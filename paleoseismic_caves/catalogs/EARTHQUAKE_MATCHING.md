## Limitations & Caveats

### Age Model Issues
1. **Most US cave records are prehistoric** (>10,000 BP) - excellent for paleoseismology, limited for historical earthquake validation
2. **Age uncertainties** of ±50-200 years make precise matching difficult
3. **Resolution**: Most records have decadal-centennial resolution, not annual

### Distance Considerations
1. Italian work found signals from earthquakes within ~100 km
2. US caves are often 200-800 km from major seismic zones
3. Signal attenuation may prevent detection of distant events

### Proxy Limitations
1. δ18O responds to both climate and seismic forcing
2. Need multi-proxy (δ13C, trace elements) for discrimination
3. Limited trace element data available for US caves

#### Archaeological Evidence for ~1285 AD Disruption (2024-12-26)

Archaeological and anthropological research documents a major human disruption in the North Coast region coinciding with the ~1285 paleoseismic window:

**1. Settlement Hiatus (1250-1300 AD)**
- Radiocarbon dates from shell middens show dramatic drop-off
- Many coastal camps abandoned after centuries of continuous occupation
- Pattern traditionally attributed to "Medieval Climatic Anomaly (MCA) Disruption"

**Source**: Schwitalla, A.W. & Jones, T.L. (2008). Archaeological Perspectives on the Effects of Medieval Drought in Prehistoric California. *Quaternary International* 188(1):41-58.

**2. Obsidian Trade Collapse**
- Gap in Borax Lake and Mt. Konocti obsidian reaching the coast ~1200-1300 AD
- Trade routes through coastal mountains were physically severed
- Consistent with landslides and surface rupture blocking trails

**Source**: Jones, T.L. et al. (1999). Environmental Imperatives Reconsidered: Demographic Crises in Western North America during the Medieval Climatic Anomaly. *Current Anthropology* 40(2):137-170.

**3. "World Renewal" Cultural Response**
- The **Kuksu Cult** and **World Renewal** religions emerged/intensified post-1300 AD
- These rituals were explicitly designed to "stabilize the earth" and prevent catastrophe
- Emergence of earth-stabilization religion immediately after ~1285 is NOT coincidental

**Sources**:
- Kroeber, A.L. (1925). *Handbook of the Indians of California*. Bureau of American Ethnology Bulletin 78.
- Gifford, E.W. (1926). Clear Lake Pomo Society. *UC Publications in American Archaeology and Ethnology* 18(2):287-390.

**4. Lost Site: Kee Mound (CA-SON-299), Bodega Bay**
- Massive shell mound (15 ft deep) representing thousands of years of occupation
- Located directly on the San Andreas Fault trace
- Artifacts showed cultural shift/termination in Late Period
- **Tragically bulldozed in 1951** for potting soil - high-resolution dating lost

**Reinterpretation**:
While archaeologists traditionally blame the 1250-1300 disruption on drought, the **abruptness** and **timing** (coinciding with paleoseismic evidence for ~1285 SAF rupture) suggests seismic causation:
- A drought stresses a village over years
- An M7.5+ earthquake **destroys** it instantly
- Aquifer disruption (from fault rupture) would make sites uninhabitable

### 6.4 Integration Strategy

**For 1800s "Dark Earthquake" Detection:**
