4. **Recovery time**: Re-equilibration takes years to decades, producing extended anomalies

The elevated Mg/Ca (+1.60σ) at the 1394 event directly reflects **enhanced deep water intrusion** through seismically-activated fracture pathways—exactly what the hypogenic origin predicts.

**Supporting Evidence**: Research on Italian karst systems notes: "Earthquakes are known to affect groundwater properties... The presence of tectonic structures (e.g., active and inactive faults) may facilitate the rise of geothermal fluids through the formation of fractures and pathways."

This scientific context transforms the Bàsura anomaly from an unexplained isotopic excursion into a **predicted response** of a hypogenic cave system to seismic fault activation.

## 4.3 The "Lost Millennium" and the "Dark Earthquake" Phenomenon

### Emanuela Guidoboni's "Lost Millennium"

**Source**: [LiveScience](https://www.livescience.com/31692-ancient-italy-eruptions.html); Guidoboni & Comastri 2005

Historical seismologist **Emanuela Guidoboni** has characterized the period from the **4th to 14th centuries** as the "lost millennium" for earthquake documentation. Key points:

- "Although researchers have uncovered much evidence about past earthquakes, there's still a significant gap in records from about the 4th century to the 14th century"
- "To piece together the seismic history of the lost millennium, researchers probably will need to turn to ancient church records, such as the Vatican Secret Archives"
- The CFTI5Med catalog covers this period through systematic archival research, but gaps remain

**Why the 1394 Event Falls in the "Lost Millennium"**:
- The 1394 event occurs at the **very end** of Guidoboni's identified gap
- The Great Western Schism (1378-1417) created administrative chaos precisely during this period
- Multiple layers of disruption (political + ecclesiastical) compounded the documentation problem

### The "Dark Earthquake" Definition

An earthquake that:
1. Actually occurred (physical evidence exists)
2. Was significant (Mw 5.5+, caused damage)
3. Left no chronicle record due to political/social disruption

## 7.5 The Onshore vs. Offshore Question

The 1887 event was **offshore** (epicenter in Ligurian Sea). The 1394 event may have been **onshore** (Toirano-Albenga hinterland).

**Onshore earthquakes are more dangerous** because:
- Shorter distance to population centers
- No water column to absorb energy
- Direct foundation damage
- Landslides in mountainous terrain

## 7.6 The "Immature Fault" Problem

Research shows (Ferrarini et al., 2021):
- **Immature faults** (less developed, lower slip rate) can produce **higher stress drops**
- Higher stress drops = **more damaging earthquakes** for a given magnitude
- The Ligurian system may be "immature" compared to Apennine faults
