The historiographical literature correctly notes that the Battle of Meloria (August 6, 1284) reshaped Mediterranean politics:

- **For Genoa**: Victory, but the *Annali* focused on naval triumph, not rural hinterland

**Critical admission from historiographical analysis:**

## Why These Events Were "Lost"

### 1. Documentary Gap

From [Stucchi et al. (2004)](https://doi.org/10.4401/ag-3330) on Italian historical earthquake completeness:

> **"The discrepancy between true seismic history and recorded seismic history is due to a combination of a documentary gap of the historical sources and to the low population and scarcity of settlements in the epicentral area."**

This quote directly describes the Toirano situation: a rural mountain hinterland with only the isolated Benedictine monastery of San Pietro dei Monti (transferred to Carthusians in 1315 after "severe decline") as a potential source of records.

### 2. Medieval Catalog Incompleteness

From [CPTI15 documentation](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10518-020-00818-y):

> **"Studies have recognized the occurrence of large magnitude events during medieval times along certain faults, but no trace is left of these major events in the historical record."**

This is exactly what Bàsura Cave reveals: physical evidence of major medieval earthquakes that left no trace in written records.

### 3. The "Dark Earthquake" Phenomenon

These two quotes from the seismological literature provide independent validation of the "Dark Earthquake" concept:

1. **The mechanism is documented**: Documentary gaps + sparse population = lost earthquakes
2. **The pattern is recognized**: Major medieval events on known faults with zero historical trace
3. **Bàsura provides the solution**: Speleothem geochemistry recovers what history lost

### 3. Political Context

**1285 CE**:
- Genoese-Pisan wars dominating chronicles
- Rural hinterland (Toirano) had sparse population
- Abbey San Pietro dei Monti was isolated Benedictine monastery (later abandoned: 1308 suppression petition, 1315 transfer to Carthusians citing need for "reconstruction")

**1394 CE**:
