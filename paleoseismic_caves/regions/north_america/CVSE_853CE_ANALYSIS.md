## Executive Summary

The 853 CE Mount Churchill eruption (Alaska) coincides precisely with Cascadia Event S (~854 CE), forming a potential TITAN-type compound event. Tree-ring evidence documents "drier northwest 852-855 CE," completing the volcanic-seismic-hydrological triad.

### 1.3 White River Ash East Lobe

The eruption emplaced the "Eastern White River Ash" (WRAe), one of the largest North American eruptions of the past 2,000 years. The ash deposit is visible >1,000 km from source and has been linked to Athapaskan migration from Yukon to the American Southwest.

## 3. Hydrological Expression: 852-855 CE Drought

### 3.1 Tree-Ring Evidence

From Mackay et al. 2022:

> "Eastern North American tree-ring moisture reconstructions are consistently dry from 852 through 855 CE. Tree-ring records in the western half of the continent reveal a mixed PDSI anomaly, generally indicating wetter conditions to the southwest and **drier in the northwest**, reminiscent of the moisture anomalies during an El Niño event in the tropical Pacific."

### 3.2 Regional Pattern

| Region | 852-855 CE Condition |
|--------|---------------------|
| Eastern North America | DRY |
| Northwestern North America | **DRY** (Oregon region) |
| Southwestern North America | Wetter |
| Pattern interpretation | El Niño-like |

The **Oregon Caves region falls in the "drier northwest" zone**, providing hydrological evidence for compound forcing.

## 6. Alternative Hypothesis: Coincidence

The null hypothesis is that the 853 CE Churchill eruption and 854 CE Cascadia Event S are unrelated:

**Arguments for coincidence**:
- Different tectonic systems (volcanic arc vs subduction zone)
- No physical mechanism linking Alaskan volcanism to Cascadia seismicity
- Cascadia events recur ~200-500 years apart; overlap with VEI 6 eruption could be random

**Arguments against coincidence**:
- The probability of a VEI 6+ eruption occurring within ±2 years of a Cascadia megathrust is low
- Even if seismically independent, the COMPOUND IMPACT on Pacific Northwest climate/hydrology is real
- The Titan concept doesn't require causal volcanic-seismic linkage, only coincident forcing
