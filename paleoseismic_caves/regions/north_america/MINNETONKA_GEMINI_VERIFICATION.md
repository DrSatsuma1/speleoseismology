## DETAILED VERIFICATION

### 1. Hecker et al. (2021) - VERIFIED ✅

**Full Citation**: Hecker, S., Schwartz, D.P., DeLong, S.B. (2021). "Rapid strain release on the Bear River fault zone, Utah–Wyoming—The impact of preexisting structure on the rupture behavior of a new normal fault." *Tectonophysics*, Volume 805, Article 228819.

**DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tecto.2021.228819

**Published**: March 15, 2021

**Key Findings VERIFIED**:
- Bear River Fault (BRF) is 44-km-long normal fault
- Surface-rupturing history began ~4500 years ago
- Consists of **at least three large events**
- Located on eastern margin of Basin and Range extension

**CANNOT VERIFY from publicly available sources**:
- Specific "Event E1" nomenclature
- Dating of 1664-1842 CE for most recent event
- Would require access to full paper text/supplementary materials

**Sources**:
- [USGS Publication Page](https://www.usgs.gov/publications/rapid-strain-release-bear-river-fault-zone-utah-wyoming-impact-preexisting-structure)
- [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0040195121001037)

### 2. Bennett et al. (2018) - VERIFIED ✅

**Full Citation**: Bennett, S.E.K., DuRoss, C.B., Gold, R.D., Briggs, R.W., Personius, S.F., Reitman, N.G., et al. (2018). "Paleoseismic Results from the Alpine Site, Wasatch Fault Zone: Timing and Displacement Data for Six Holocene Earthquakes at the Salt Lake City–Provo Segment Boundary." *Bulletin of the Seismological Society of America*, 108(6): 3202-3224.

**Key Findings VERIFIED**:
- Study location: Alpine, Utah (segment boundary)
- **Six earthquakes occurred ~6.2–0.4 ka**
- Most recent event (AL1): **~0.4 ka** (roughly **1600 CE or later**)
- Bayesian modeling of 13 radiocarbon + 13 OSL ages

**NOTE**: The paper focuses on the **segment boundary**, not specifically the Provo segment interior. The 0.4 ka age broadly supports the Gemini claim of "0.2-0.4 ka (1600-1800 CE)" but is NOT as precise as "1625 CE."

**ASSESSMENT**: Gemini's claim that this validates 1625-1630 CE is **OVERSTATED**. The 0.4 ka date has uncertainty ranges that likely span several centuries.

**Sources**:
- [USGS Publication](https://www.usgs.gov/publications/paleoseismic-results-alpine-site-wasatch-fault-zone-timing-and-displacement-data-six)
- [Abstract accessible at USGS](https://pubs.usgs.gov/publication/70200441)

### 3. Colman (2006) - VERIFIED ✅

**Full Citation**: Colman, S.M. (2006). "Acoustic stratigraphy of Bear Lake, Utah-Idaho: late Quaternary sedimentation patterns in a simple half-graben." *Sedimentary Geology*, 185: 113-125.

**Key Findings VERIFIED**:
- 277-km network of seismic-reflection profiles collected
- Basin developed as simple half-graben with steep normal fault on east
- **"Several faults cut the youngest sediments in the lake as well as the modern lake floor"**
- Secondary normal faults west of master fault imaged
- Oldest mapped reflector: ~100 ka

**ASSESSMENT**: Gemini's claim about "modern lake floor faults" is **ACCURATE**.

**Sources**:
- [USGS Publication](https://pubs.usgs.gov/publication/70030695)
- [University of Nebraska Digital Commons](https://digitalcommons.unl.edu/usgsstaffpub/276/)

### 4. Bear River Fault Event Chronology - PARTIALLY VERIFIED ⚠️

**VERIFIED**:
- Surface-rupturing history began ~4500 years ago (Hecker 2021)
- At least **3 large events** documented (Hecker 2021)
- USGS Quaternary Fault Database (Fault 2364) reports most recent event at **~2.1 ka** (McCalpin 2003)

**CANNOT VERIFY**:
- Specific "Event E1" nomenclature
- Dating to **1664-1842 CE** for most recent event
- The USGS database shows most recent event at 2.1 ka (~100 BCE), **NOT 1664-1842 CE**

**CRITICAL DISCREPANCY**: The publicly accessible USGS Quaternary Fault Database shows the most recent event at ~2,100 years ago, which **contradicts** Gemini's claim of a 1664-1842 CE event.

**RECOMMENDATION**: This discrepancy needs resolution before accepting Gemini's attribution of the 1680 CE speleothem signal to Bear River Fault.

**Sources**:
- [USGS Quaternary Fault Database - Fault 2364](https://earthquake.usgs.gov/cfusion/qfault/show_report_AB_archive.cfm?fault_id=2364&section_id=c)
- [Hecker et al. 2021](https://www.usgs.gov/publications/rapid-strain-release-bear-river-fault-zone-utah-wyoming-impact-preexisting-structure)

### 5. Malad City Segment - PARTIALLY VERIFIED ⚠️

**VERIFIED**:
- Wasatch Fault has 10 segments: 5 active medial, 5 less active distal
- Distal segments have **mostly pre-Holocene surface ruptures**
- Distal segments have slip rates **<0.5 mm/year**
- Distal segments have recurrence intervals **≥10,000 years**
- Malad City segment is "relatively unstudied" and "received much less attention"

**CANNOT EXPLICITLY VERIFY**:
- That Malad City specifically is dormant for **entire Holocene (>10,000 years)**
- No specific paleoseismic study data found for Malad City segment

**ASSESSMENT**: Likely correct based on characterization as "distal segment," but not explicitly stated.

**Sources**:
- [Utah Geological Survey - End Segments](https://geology.utah.gov/map-pub/survey-notes/wasatch-fault-end-segments/)
- [USGS Wasatch Fault Zone Segmentation](https://pubs.usgs.gov/publication/70016367)

### 7. Provo Segment MRE Timing - VERIFIED ✅ (with caveats)

**VERIFIED from Bennett et al. 2018**:
- Most recent event at Alpine site (segment boundary): **~0.4 ka**
- Six earthquakes: ~6.2–0.4 ka
- 0.4 ka = approximately **1600 CE or later**

**ADDITIONAL CONTEXT from DuRoss et al. 2016**:
- American Fork site (northern Provo): earthquake at ~0.4 ka
- Better-constrained Provo events at Rock Creek/Mapleton: **0.6 ± 0.07 ka**
- Uncertainty about whether 0.4 ka represents full Provo MRE or partial rupture

**ASSESSMENT**: The broad timing of "1600-1800 CE" is **REASONABLE** based on 0.4 ka ages, but Gemini's precision of "1625-1630 CE" is **OVERSTATED**. The 0.4 ka date likely has ±100-200 year uncertainties.
