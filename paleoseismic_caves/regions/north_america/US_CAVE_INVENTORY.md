## Priority Caves for Paleoseismic Analysis

### ⭐ Tier 0: VALIDATED SEISMIC DETECTIONS
1. **Oregon Caves NM** - ✅ 3 Cascadia megathrusts confirmed (T5, Event S, Event W)
   - Best candidate for extending historical coverage (ends 1687 CE)
   - Additional sampling of stalagmite tips could capture 1700 M9.0
2. **Minnetonka Cave** (ID) - ⚠️ 2 probable Wasatch Fault matches (1680, 1625-1630 CE)
   - Only cave with clear historical anomalies (1527-2003 CE)

### Tier 1: Caves with Multi-Proxy Data
1. **Abaco Island Cave** (Bahamas) - δ18O, δ13C, Sr/Ca - 4 entities
2. **Lake Shasta Cave** (CA) - δ18O, δ13C, Mg/Ca - ⚠️ PREHISTORIC ONLY (ends 12,239 BCE)
3. **Lehman Caves** (NV) - δ18O, Mg/Ca - Great Basin seismic zone
4. **Larga Cave** (PR) - δ18O, Mg/Ca - Puerto Rico Trench

### Tier 2: Caves Near Major Seismic Zones
1. **Devils Hole** (NV) - near Walker Lane, 4 entities, iconic paleoclimate record
2. **Cave of the Bells** (AZ) - near Basin & Range faults
3. **Fort Stanton Cave** (NM) - Rio Grande Rift
4. **Leviathan Cave** (NV) - Central Nevada Seismic Belt
5. **DeSoto Caverns** (AL) - nearest to New Madrid Zone (800km)
6. **Buckeye Creek** (WV) - Eastern US seismicity, multiple entities

### Tier 3: Long Records (All Prehistoric)
1. **Cold Water Cave** (IA) - Holocene record
2. **Moaning Cave** (CA) - ⚠️ Sierra Nevada foothills, NO AGE MODEL
3. **El Capitan Cave** (AK) - Alaska subduction zone

### ❌ NOT SUITABLE for 1800s Earthquake Detection
California caves (McLean's, Moaning, White Moon, Lake Shasta) are all prehistoric.
For 1800s "dark earthquakes," use alternative proxies:
- Tree rings: Fort Ross/Gualala redwoods (1397-2023 CE)
- Lake sediments: Pallett Creek, Lake Tahoe
- See EARTHQUAKE_MATCHING.md Section 6 for data sources
