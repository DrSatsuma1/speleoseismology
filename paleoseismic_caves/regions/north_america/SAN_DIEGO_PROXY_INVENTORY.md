### Cross-Site Correlation: The 1855 Signal

**CRITICAL FINDING**: Both Mt. Laguna and Palomar Mountain show anomalies at 1855 CE, exactly 7 years before the documented 1862 Rose Canyon earthquake (M~6):

| Site | 1855 Z-score | Distance to Rose Canyon |
|------|--------------|------------------------|
| Mt. Laguna | +1.87σ | 55 km |
| Palomar | **+2.05σ** | 75 km |

**Interpretation**: The enhancement pattern (positive z) suggests **strain release effect** rather than direct damage. This is consistent with:
1. A precursory event ~1855 that released strain
2. Post-seismic growth enhancement following local stress changes
3. Regional groundwater/hydrological response to Rose Canyon activity

**Comparison to 1862 Event**:
| Year | Mt. Laguna | Palomar | Pattern |
|------|------------|---------|---------|
| 1855 | **+1.87** | **+2.05** | DUAL ENHANCEMENT |
| 1857 | +1.55 | +0.86 | Single site |
| 1862 | -0.93 | N/A | Suppression at epicenter year |

The **dual-site enhancement at 1855** is stronger than the year of the earthquake itself, suggesting either:
- A precursor event in 1855
- Strain buildup releasing through creep
- Volcanic/climatic false positive (requires discrimination)

## 5. San Diego Trough Marine Sediments

### ⚠️ CRITICAL GAP: Offshore Paleoseismology Unknown

From **Ryan et al. (2012)**, peer-reviewed in *Bulletin of the Seismological Society of America*:

> "Along with most faults offshore southern California, **the slip rate and paleoseismic history of the SDTFZ are unknown**."

This is a **recognized research gap** in the literature - not an inference.

---

### San Diego Trough Fault Zone (SDTFZ)

**Primary Reference**: Ryan HF, Conrad JE, Paull CK, McGann M (2012). Slip rate on the San Diego trough fault zone, inner California Borderland, and the 1986 Oceanside earthquake swarm revisited. *Bulletin of the Seismological Society of America* 102(6):2300-2312. DOI: [10.1785/0120110317](https://doi.org/10.1785/0120110317)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Length** | 340 km (extends 60 km further north than previously mapped) |
| **Slip Rate** | 1.5 ± 0.3 mm/yr (past 12,270 years) |
| **Plate Motion Share** | ~25% of Inner California Borderland slip |
| **Earthquake Potential** | M7.6-7.9 (multi-segment rupture) |
| **Holocene Activity** | Confirmed |
| **Paleoseismic History** | **UNKNOWN** |

**Key Finding**: The fault has a 5-km-wide stepover where the 1986 Oceanside earthquake swarm occurred. Turbidites in San Diego Trough record "late Quaternary deformation" but **no paleoseismic dating has been performed**.

---

### La Jolla Canyon Turbidite Record

**Primary Reference**: Paull CK, Caress DW, Lundsten E, Gwiazda R, Anderson K, McGann M, Conrad J, Edwards B, Sumner EJ (2013). Anatomy of the La Jolla Submarine Canyon system; offshore southern California. *Marine Geology* 335:16-34. DOI: [10.1016/j.margeo.2012.10.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.margeo.2012.10.003)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Location** | Offshore La Jolla, San Diego County |
| **Method** | AUV multibeam sonar, ROV sampling, vibracores |
| **Turbidites** | Thin (<10 cm) turbidites on terrace surfaces |
| **Age** | Recent (modern plant material in sands confirms active transport) |
| **Sediment Stability** | Sand liquefied during vibracore collection |
| **Pre-canyon strata age** | <1.232 million years (Quaternary) |
| **Earthquake Dating** | **NONE PERFORMED** |

**Gap**: Turbidites exist and are recent, but **no radiocarbon dating or earthquake correlation** has been attempted. This is exactly the type of record that Goldfinger et al. (2003) used successfully in Cascadia/Northern SAF.

---

### San Diego Bay Pull-Apart Basin

**Primary Reference**: Singleton DM, Maloney JM, Brothers DS, Klotsko S, Driscoll NW, Rockwell TK (2021). Recency of Faulting and Subsurface Architecture of the San Diego Bay Pull-Apart Basin, California, USA. *Frontiers in Earth Science* 9:641346. DOI: [10.3389/feart.2021.641346](https://doi.org/10.3389/feart.2021.641346)

**Key Findings**:
- San Diego Bay is a **double pull-apart basin** between Rose Canyon, Descanso, and San Miguel-Vallecitos faults
- Complex network of down-to-the-east normal faults beneath the bay
- High-resolution Chirp and MCS data available through USGS NAMSS
- Coronado Bridge borehole transect provides radiocarbon age constraints

---

### Implications for Speleothem Research

This offshore gap makes **alternative proxies (speleothems, tree rings) even more valuable** for San Diego paleoseismology:

1. **No speleothems** in San Diego County (no karst)
2. **Tree rings** are the only proxy with temporal resolution
3. **Offshore turbidites** could validate findings but require new coring + dating
4. **Our 1855 tree ring anomaly** is currently the strongest paleoseismic proxy signal for Rose Canyon

**Opportunity**: If tree ring or other proxy evidence identifies candidate earthquake dates, future offshore coring could test these hypotheses in La Jolla Canyon / San Diego Trough turbidite record.

## 8. Key Findings

### Strong Evidence

1. **1855 Dual-Site Tree Ring Anomaly**: Both Mt. Laguna (z=+1.87) and Palomar (z=+2.05) show enhancement 7 years before the 1862 Rose Canyon M~6 earthquake. This is the strongest local signal in the San Diego tree ring record that correlates with a Rockwell event.

2. **Seal Beach E1 Event**: Coseismic subsidence at 632-363 cal BP (~1385-1654 CE) on the NIRCFZ. This overlaps our 1580 tree ring anomaly and Carrizo Plain paleoseismic, suggesting possible multi-fault cascade.

### Data Gaps

1. **No speleothems** in San Diego County (no karst)
2. **No paleoseismic lake cores** from Cuyamaca or Henshaw
3. **Tree ring record doesn't extend to ~1300 AD** Rose Canyon event
4. **Weak signal for 1750s events** (too distant? or different fault behavior?)

### Recommendations

1. **Collaborate with Rockwell** on comparing our 1855 tree ring signal to his Rose Canyon stratigraphy
2. **Propose lake coring** at Cuyamaca/Henshaw for turbidite analysis
3. **Extend tree ring collection** to older stumps (pre-1640) for ~1300 AD coverage
4. **Analyze Seal Beach E1** radiocarbon more closely for 1580 correlation
