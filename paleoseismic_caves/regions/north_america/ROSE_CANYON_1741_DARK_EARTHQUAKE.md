# The 1741 Rose Canyon Dark Earthquake Hypothesis

**Date**: 2024-12-30
**Status**: PROBABLE (Tier 2 evidence)
**Location**: Rose Canyon Fault, San Diego, California
**Proposed Magnitude**: M5.5-6.5

## Executive Summary

Analysis of San Diego tree ring chronologies reveals a **dual-site suppression signal at 1741 CE** that may represent an undocumented Rose Canyon Fault earthquake occurring approximately 4-9 years before Rockwell's documented "mid-1700s" paleoseismic event.

### Key Evidence

| Proxy | Site | Distance | 1741 Signal | Pattern |
|-------|------|----------|-------------|---------|
| Tree rings | Mt. Laguna | 55 km | **z=-1.09** | Suppression |
| Tree rings | Palomar Mt. | 75 km | **z=-1.10** | Suppression |
| Speleothem | Crystal Cave | 450 km | z=+2.33 | Enhancement |

### Critical Finding: Regional Divergence

The **opposite-sign responses** at San Diego (suppression) vs Sequoia (enhancement) rule out volcanic forcing, which would produce uniform suppression globally. This divergence pattern indicates a **local San Diego seismic event**.

- **Result**: 1739 Tarumae not explicitly listed in major eruption catalogs
- The eruption may not have produced a bipolar sulfate signal
- Northern Hemisphere-only signal would have limited global impact

### Critical Discrimination: The Divergence Pattern

| Proxy | Location | 1741 Signal | If Volcanic (Expected) | Actual |
|-------|----------|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Tree rings | San Diego | z=-1.09 | Suppression | Suppression |
| Speleothem | Sequoia | z=+2.33 | Suppression | **ENHANCEMENT** |

**The Sequoia ENHANCEMENT is the key discriminator:**

- Volcanic cooling → global suppression everywhere
- San Diego DOWN + Sequoia UP = **regional divergence**
- This pattern is consistent with LOCAL seismic damage in San Diego, NOT global volcanic forcing

### Additional Counter-Evidence

1. **Timing**: 1738 suppression predates August 1739 eruption (wrong order)
2. **Duration**: Crystal Cave enhancement persists 14 years (1734-1749) - too long for volcanic
3. **Peak timing**: Crystal Cave peak at 1745 (z=+2.84) is 6 years after eruption - wrong lag for volcanic

**Conclusion**: The 1741 San Diego tree ring suppression is **unlikely to be volcanic**.

## 4. Crystal Cave Contemporaneous Signal

### Sequoia Response (450 km from San Diego)

| Year CE | δ18O (‰) | Z-score | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| 1738.0 | -8.28 | +1.86 | Rising |
| **1741.6** | **-8.09** | **+2.33** | **Contemporaneous** |
| 1745.4 | -7.88 | +2.84 | Peak |
| 1749.0 | -8.22 | +2.00 | Declining |

### Interpretation

Crystal Cave (Sequoia) is too distant (450 km) to directly detect a Rose Canyon M6 event. However, the **opposite-direction response** (enhancement vs suppression) supports the seismic interpretation:

**Scenario A: Cascade/Regional Stress Pulse**
- 1741 Rose Canyon rupture → regional stress change
- Stress transfer affects Sierra Nevada faults
- Crystal Cave detects LOCAL Sierra response to regional forcing

**Scenario B: Independent Events**
- San Diego: 1741 local Rose Canyon event
- Sierra Nevada: 1741-1745 local Kern Canyon or SNFF event
- Both represent regional tectonic activation ~1740s

Either scenario supports the earthquake hypothesis and rules out volcanic.
