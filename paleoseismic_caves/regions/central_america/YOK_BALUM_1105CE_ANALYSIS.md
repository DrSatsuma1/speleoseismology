# The 1105-1125 CE Anomaly at Yok Balum: Volcanic Aftermath

## Executive Summary

**FINDING**: The 1105-1125 CE event (peak z = -2.98) is caused by **volcanic forcing** from the **1108 CE Unknown Eruption** - the **5th largest volcanic event of the last millennium** (19.16 Tg S).

**KEY DISCRIMINATION**: Like the 1228 CE and 1274 CE anomalies, the 1105-1125 CE event shows **decoupled proxy behavior**:
- δ18O: z = -2.98 (STRONG)
- δ13C: z ≈ -1.3 (MODERATE)

This **δ18O-dominant pattern** is characteristic of **volcanic climate forcing**, not seismic aquifer disruption.

## Volcanic Forcing Mechanism

### Why δ18O Shows Strong Response

1. **Sulfate aerosols** → stratospheric cooling → tropospheric reorganization
2. **ITCZ migration** → altered moisture source regions → isotopically depleted precipitation
3. **Enhanced effective moisture** → increased cave drip rate

### Why δ13C Shows Only Moderate Response

Volcanic forcing does NOT strongly affect δ13C because:
- Soil CO₂ production (biogenic) is temperature-dependent but not immediately disrupted
- No deep aquifer mixing or flowpath disruption
- No geogenic CO₂ introduction from fault activity

**This decoupling is diagnostic of climatic (volcanic) vs. seismic forcing.**
