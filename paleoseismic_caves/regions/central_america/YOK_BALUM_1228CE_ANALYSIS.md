# The 1228-1238 CE Anomaly at Yok Balum: Volcanic Aftermath

## Executive Summary

**FINDING**: The 1228-1238 CE event (peak z = -2.93) is caused by **volcanic forcing** from the **1230 CE Unknown Eruption** - the **4th largest volcanic event of the last millennium** (23.78 Tg S).

**KEY DISCRIMINATION**: Like the 1274 CE anomaly, the 1228-1238 CE event shows **decoupled proxy behavior**:
- δ18O: z = -2.93 (STRONG)
- δ13C: z = -1.91 (MODERATE)

This **δ18O-dominant pattern** is characteristic of **volcanic climate forcing**, not seismic aquifer disruption.

## Volcanic Forcing Mechanism

1. **Sulfate aerosols** → atmospheric cooling → isotopically lighter precipitation (depleted δ18O)
2. **Reduced evaporation** → increased effective moisture → cave drip rate increase
3. **Carbon cycle LESS affected** → moderate δ13C response (biogenic CO₂ unchanged)
4. **Hydrological reorganization** → multi-year isotopic response

### Why δ13C Shows Only Moderate Response

Volcanic forcing affects δ18O through:
- Temperature-dependent fractionation of precipitation
- Shift in moisture source regions

Volcanic forcing does NOT strongly affect δ13C because:
- Soil CO₂ production (biogenic) is unchanged
- No deep aquifer mixing or flowpath disruption
- No geogenic CO₂ introduction

**This decoupling is diagnostic of climatic vs. seismic forcing.**

## Conclusion and Classification

### Reasoning

1. The **1230 CE volcanic eruption** (23.78 Tg S) is the **4th largest of the last millennium**
