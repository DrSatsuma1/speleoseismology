# The 1159-1178 CE Anomaly at Yok Balum: TITAN II (Belize)

## Executive Summary

**CLASSIFICATION**: **TITAN II** - The second confirmed Titan Event (after 1285 Italy)

**FINDING**: The 1159-1178 CE event is a **COMPOUND EVENT** consisting of two distinct pulses with different signatures:

1. **Pulse 1 (1159-1163 CE)**: **SEISMIC** - Coupled δ18O/δ13C response (ratio 1.51)
2. **Pulse 2 (1174-1182 CE)**: **VOLCANIC** - Decoupled response following 1171 CE eruption

This pattern mirrors the 1285 CE "Titan Event" in Italy (volcanic-seismic coupling).

**Why TITAN II** (designated 2024-12-30):
- ✅ **Volcanic component**: 1171 CE eruption (18.05 Tg S, #4 of High Medieval Period)
- ✅ **Seismic component**: Pulse 1 shows COUPLED δ18O/δ13C (ratio 1.51 = seismic signature)
- ✅ **Two-pulse structure**: Matches TITAN I (1285 Italy) compound pattern

## Compound Event Interpretation

### Proposed Sequence

1. **~1159 CE**: Unknown earthquake in Motagua Fault zone
   - Causes aquifer disruption
   - COUPLED δ18O + δ13C response
   - Similar to 620 CE pattern

2. **1163-1173 CE**: Recovery period
   - Values return to near-normal
   - Aquifer stabilizes

3. **1171 CE**: Major volcanic eruption (18.05 Tg S)
   - Tropical stratospheric event
   - ITCZ reorganization

4. **1174-1182 CE**: Post-volcanic climate response
   - DECOUPLED response (δ18O >> δ13C)
   - Typical volcanic aftermath

## Archaeological Context

### 12th Century Maya (Postclassic)

The 1159-1163 CE seismic pulse coincides with:
- **Late Early Postclassic** (1000-1200 CE)
- **Chichen Itza decline** begins ~1150-1200 CE
- Transition to **Mayapan** dominance

No specific archaeological evidence of earthquake destruction has been documented for this period, but the Early→Late Postclassic transition is poorly understood.

### Potential Archaeological Signatures

If a major earthquake occurred ~1159 CE, we might expect:
- Building damage at lowland sites
- Trade route disruption
- Population movements

**Priority**: Literature search for 12th-century Maya site disruptions.
