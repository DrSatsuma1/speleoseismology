## Executive Summary

The Kocain Cave (SW Turkey) δ18O anomaly at **415.04 CE** correlates with the historically documented **417 CE Cibyra earthquake** (MSK 9) on the Fethiye-Burdur fault zone. This is a **METHODOLOGY VALIDATION** - the speleothem successfully detected a known historical earthquake.

**This is NOT a dark earthquake candidate** - it confirms the detection methodology works.

## Archaeological Evidence at Cibyra

From Akyüz & Altunel (2001), "Geological and archaeological evidence for post-Roman earthquake surface faulting at Cibyra, SW Turkey":

1. **Stadium rows**: 50 cm sinistral (left-lateral) offset
2. **Building damage**: Broken corners, collapsed walls, broken columns
3. **Tilted blocks**: Toppled construction elements in city center
4. **Fault traces**: Surface rupture visible through archaeological site

**Interpretation**: The archaeological damage pattern indicates a major surface-rupturing earthquake consistent with MSK 9 intensity.

## The 297-Year Recovery Anomaly

The extremely long recovery time (296.5 years) is unusual and requires explanation:

### Possible Interpretations

1. **Multiple earthquakes merged**: The Fethiye-Burdur fault zone has recurrence intervals of ~100-200 years; subsequent events may have prolonged the anomaly
2. **Permanent aquifer damage**: Major surface rupture may have permanently altered karst hydrology
3. **Continuous seismic activity**: SW Turkey shows persistent M5+ activity even in modern times (69 events 2000-2025)
4. **Dating artifact**: Multiple anomalies incorrectly attributed to single event

### Modern Seismicity Context

USGS catalog (2000-2025) within 200 km of Kocain Cave:
- **M6+**: 4 events (M6.6 Kos 2017, M6.5 Sultandağı 2002, M6.0 Ölüdeniz 2012, M6.0 Akşehir 2000)
- **M5+**: 69 events total
- **Depth range**: 5-135 km (mostly shallow crustal)

This region is **EXTREMELY seismically active** - the long recovery may reflect cumulative effects of multiple events.
