# Sofular Cave (Turkey) - Dark Earthquake Analysis

## Cave Information
- **Location**: 41.4167°N, 31.9333°E (Black Sea coast, Turkey)
- **Seismic context**: North Anatolian Fault Zone - one of world's most active strike-slip faults
- **Record**: 1000 BCE to 1900 CE (510 samples)
- **SISAL entities**: 305 (SO-1), 687 (SO-4)

### ~402 CE Dark Earthquake Hypothesis

**The ~395-410 CE cluster represents a potential undocumented NAF earthquake:**

1. **Strong coupled signal**: δ18O +2.91σ + δ13C +1.58σ = seismic signature
2. **Timing gap**: 45 years BEFORE documented 447 CE Constantinople earthquake
3. **Duration**: ~15-year anomaly (395-410 CE) suggests major event
4. **No known historical event**: No Byzantine records for this period

**Historical context**:
- 358 CE: Nicomedia earthquake (documented, ~80 km from Sofular)
- 447 CE: Constantinople earthquake (documented)
- **~402 CE: NO DOCUMENTED EVENT** → Dark earthquake candidate

**Proposed magnitude**: M6.5-7.0 (based on signal strength comparable to documented events)
