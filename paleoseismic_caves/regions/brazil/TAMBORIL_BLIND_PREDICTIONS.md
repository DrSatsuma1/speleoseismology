# TAMBORIL CAVE BLIND PREDICTIONS

**LOCKED: 2024-12-31**
**Entity**: 97 (TM0), Brazil
**Location**: -21.1°S, -56.6°W (Mato Grosso do Sul, Brazil)
**Tectonic setting**: Intraplate - São Francisco Craton edge
**Analysis window**: 108 - 1966 CE (472 samples)

## Methodology

Anomalies classified BEFORE checking earthquake catalogs using:
1. **Coupling ratio**: |δ18O_z| / |δ13C_z| - values near 1.0-2.0 = SEISMIC (coupled), >3.0 = CLIMATIC (decoupled)
2. **δ13C absolute values**: > -9.0‰ = geogenic CO₂ (seismic indicator)
3. **Multi-year clusters**: Consecutive anomalies suggest real events
4. **Intraplate context**: Brazilian earthquakes are rare (M < 5.5 typical), long recurrence

---

## IMPORTANT: Intraplate Seismic Context

Brazil is seismically quiet but NOT aseismic:
- **2007 Itacarambi M4.9**: ~300 km from Tamboril - modern reference
- **Historical**: Sparse catalog before 1900
- **Recurrence**: Centuries between significant events
- **Magnitude**: Typically M4.0-5.5 maximum

If anomalies match known seismicity → validates method in new setting.
If anomalies have NO matches → potential "dark earthquake" discovery.

## Key Insight: Positive vs Negative Anomalies

**Events C and D** (867 CE, 1006 CE) show POSITIVE δ18O and δ13C anomalies (less negative than baseline).
- This is **opposite** to most seismic signals in the literature
- Interpretation: Intraplate earthquakes may produce DIFFERENT signatures than plate-boundary events
- Positive δ18O = warmer/dryer conditions OR
- Positive δ13C = geogenic CO₂ influx (seismic indicator)
- The TIGHT COUPLING is the key discriminant

**Events A and B** (1527 CE, 1760 CE) show negative δ18O with moderate negative δ13C.
- More typical "wet" anomaly pattern
- Coupling suggests seismic despite negative direction
